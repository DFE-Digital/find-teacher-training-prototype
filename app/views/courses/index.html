About the course

You'll train to teach Key Stages 4 and 5. You'll spend the majority of time in a school, learning on the job. You'll also attend classes at the university campus in Hornsey. The amount of time you spend teaching will gradually increase over the year. In school you'll work closely with a mentor and an initial teacher training (ITT) coordinator. Over 95% of people who train with UNEL get a teaching job in their first year.

Requirements

Qualifications

You'll need a degree: 2:2 or higher, ideally in business studies. Ideally you'll have at least a B in A-Level business studies.

Personal qualities

You'll need to demonstrate confidence in using computers and other technology in the classroom. You'l also need to be able to pick up new skills quickly.

Other requirements

If you're successful in your application, you'll need to do the following before you start the course: demonstrate an ability to communicate clearly and grammatically in spoken and written English; have at least 2 weeks' experience in a school, including observing lessons taking place; pass the skills test in literacy and numeracy; apply for Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) security checks.

Placement schools

Candidates will, where possible, be placed at Biddulph High School for one of their placements. Their second placement will take into consideration the place they live, so that travel time can be kept to a minimum.

We have strong links across both Staffordshire and South Cheshire, so that you can get a wide teaching experience taking size and catchment areas of schools into consideration.

You will start with 3 days a week in your first placement school from September to Christmas, with 50% teaching time. You will then begin at your second placement on 4 days a week from Christmas until Easter, with 50% teaching time. For the final block, you will be in schools for the full 5 days, with a commitment of 50% teaching time.

About the training provider

We make our decisions to offer a place based on your enthusiasm, aptitude and commitment to teaching as demonstrated by your application form, qualifications, experience, reference and performance at the interview.

Access for disabled people

The university is committed to supporting trainees with disabilities. Contact the university if you would like them to make any adjustments during your application, interview process and training programme. The campus has a ramp for wheelchair access to its front entrance, as well as a lift to all floors.

Interview process

If you're shortlisted you'll get an initial interview at the university, which will give you the chance to demonstrate your skills and experience.

The most successful applicants will be those who have focused their personal statements on why they want to teach and given examples of what they can bring to the teaching profession.

As part of the selection process you must pass 2 skills tests in literacy and numeracy. You should register before the interview.

After you've qualified

All shortlisted candidates will be invited for an interview day, generally held at UNEL but sometimes at partnership schools too. As there are limited dates available for us to interview and a fixed number of places we can offer, we may not be able to offer you another date if you cannot attend the interview day.
